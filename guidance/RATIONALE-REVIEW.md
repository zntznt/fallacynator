# Philosopher Soundness Review — HOW-IT-DECIDES & WHY-THESE-WEIGHTS

_4 lenses (logician, Bayesian epistemologist, argumentation theorist, philosopher of disagreement) → 31 objections → adversarial verify → 19 upheld → synthesis._

## Verdict: **sound-with-fixes**

> The docs are substantively honest about the engine's mechanics, but three claims overclaim what is verified or are flatly falsified by the live code, and a handful of analogies/labels equivocate in ways a philosopher would flag; everything is fixable with targeted wording (no engine changes).

## Should-clarify

1. The label 'VALID' silently carries the technical weight of logical validity/soundness while the engine checks neither. The prose attaches 'sound'/'assumed-sound' to the VALID side (doc1 line 32) and the engine never tests whether the conclusion follows or premises are true (no such check exists in engine.js). But do NOT rename the hypothesis: the code's real valid_earned vs cynic_valid split (engine.js:489–493) would make NO_FALLACY_FOUND/NOT_DEFEATED inaccurate for the earned branch.
   - HOW-IT-DECIDES.md: (a) line 32, change 'assumed-sound' to 'given the benefit of the doubt'; (b) add one sentence near line 29: 'Note: VALID here is just the name of the no-fallacy hypothesis — not logical validity or soundness. The app never checks whether the conclusion follows from the premises or whether they are true; it estimates whether one catalogued fallacy is present and, for the earned outcome, whether you affirmed real virtues.' Keep 'holds up' as the plain gloss. The '(doc1: never sound)' citation in objection 1 should be disregarded — line 124's 'never sound' is the doc correctly declining to claim soundness.

2. 'innocent until proven otherwise' (line 29) and the surrounding line-32 prose ('the goodwill thesis, encoded as a number'; answers must 'earn' a verdict) let the charity read as literal good faith about the arguer, when 60% is a tunable knob (PRIOR_VALID, floor 0.55 via G7) and the engine never models the arguer.
   - At line 29, anchor the metaphor and its knob-nature once: '…a deliberately high starting prior on \"no fallacy is present\" (PRIOR_VALID, a tunable value with a 0.55 floor enforced by validateBank G7). We borrow courtroom language — innocent until proven otherwise — as a charitable stance toward the person making the argument, not a literal property of the argument.' At line 32, locate the charity in the procedure: 'the engine starts the VALID hypothesis ahead, so the evidence in your answers has to outweigh that head start before any fallacy is named.' Do not rewrite for the category-error charge — the scare-quoted legal idiom is standard and misleads no one about engine behavior.

3. Local-vs-global leap in the 👍 bullet. doc1 line 94 says '👍 = evidence the argument is fine' while the parallel line 95 correctly scopes 👎 to 'the fallacy'. A 👍 is applied (as answer 'no') to only the one fallacy whose tell list contains that qid (engine.js:522–524), so it is local evidence against one fallacy, not global 'fine' — in tension with the per-fallacy isolation the next section (Step 3.3) is built to enforce.
   - Change line 94 to: '👍 = \"it does the good thing\" = evidence against the specific fallacy this question targets — not, by itself, that the argument is fine overall.' Do not also import a 'fine means only no-fallacy-detected' clause; the doc already uses 'fine' consistently for the aggregate VALID verdict and the four-outcome table distinguishes valid_earned from cynic_valid.

4. Court analogy at line 143 inverts the direction of doubt. Legal reasonable doubt favors the defendant (the argument, cast as innocent everywhere else in the doc); the 'vague doubt' here is scattered 👎 (denied tells), which are evidence AGAINST the argument (engine.js:523), and dispersed denials route to inconclusive_lean (line 544–546). So anti-argument evidence is labeled 'doubt' that supposedly favors the argument — an internal inconsistency. Separately, the same analogy over-extends onto the runner-up gate (objection 13): beating innocence is genuinely standard-of-proof-like, but RATIO_RUNNERUP=2.5 is label-disambiguation, and a court does not require the charge to dominate alternative charges — so a definitely-fallacious argument can return 'not sure' purely because two labels tie.
   - Replace line 143's 'This is the same principle as a court: lots of vague doubt isn't a conviction.' with text that separates the two gates and keeps the directional honesty, e.g.: 'Two different things are going on. (a) Beating innocence is a genuine standard of proof — like a court, the prior is \"it holds up\" and the evidence must decisively overcome it. (b) Beating the runner-up fallacy by 2.5× is NOT courtroom logic: a court convicts on the proven elements of the charged offense regardless of whether another charge also fits, whereas here, if two fallacy labels are close, we decline to name one. So an argument can decisively beat innocence — be genuinely fallacious — and still come back \"not enough to be sure\" purely because the label is ambiguous. And unlike a court, scattered failures across different fallacies do not earn a clean pass: two or more independently nicked fallacies yield a hedged lean toward the strongest (engine.js:544–546), not \"holds up.\"' (Folds objections 5, 13, and 16 into one corrected passage.)

5. doc1 introduces the lr table as 'likelihood-ratio' weights without doc2's caveat that they were reverse-engineered to hit the gate (doc2 lines 71, 148), so doc1 alone borrows the credibility of an estimated statistic for what are tuned decision weights. Mechanically the term is accurate (they play the LR role in update(), engine.js:314–320); the gap is only doc1 omitting doc2's honesty.
   - In HOW-IT-DECIDES.md around line 39, add: 'These enter the update like likelihood ratios — \"yes\" multiplies that verdict's odds by the cell value — but their values were set to hit the accusation gate, not estimated from data on how fallacious-vs-sound arguments answer. We do not claim 4.5 = P(yes|fallacy)/P(yes|VALID); see WHY-THESE-WEIGHTS.md.' No doc2 change needed.

6. Per-fallacy scores are not one joint distribution, but Step 2's 'sums to 100' / 'belief update' framing can carry forward. Each fallacy is normalized in its own {VALID,F} world (Z = raw[fid]+raw.VALID, engine.js:527, different denominator per fallacy) and VALID shown is the min isolated share (line 535–537); top.p vs runner.p is a severity ranking, not a joint-probability comparison. The doc already scopes 'sums to 100' to the sequential interview and documents the isolation, so only an explicit flag is missing.
   - Add after Step 3 item 3 (HOW-IT-DECIDES.md, after line 102): 'Note: these per-fallacy scores are NOT one probability distribution. Each is normalized inside its own two-hypothesis {it's-fine, this-fallacy} world — deliberately, so siblings can't dilute each other — and the VALID figure shown is the strongest fallacy's own \"it's fine\" share. Treat them as a comparable severity ranking, not joint credences. The \"sums to 100\" / belief-update description in Step 2 is the sequential interview's mechanism.'

7. The self-consistent gate↔weights loop (doc2 lines 71, 93–95) reads as two independent justifications. The 0.06/0.18 window endpoints are functions of the 4.5/0.3 weights (CONFIG comment engine.js:42–51 confirms 0.12 is the window midpoint), so there is one free parameter (the charity target), not two anchors. The doc already disclaims per-number pedigree and names the external check, so KEEP 'the math, not a vibe'; just name the single degree of freedom.
   - Add after line 95: 'These two readings are not independent justifications — they are one constraint viewed from both ends. There is exactly one free choice: the charity target \"two denials convict.\" Given it, the gate (0.12) and the weights (4.5/0.3) each imply the other; the 0.06/0.18 endpoints are functions of those weights. This is principled internal calibration made decidable by arithmetic rather than eyeballed — that is what \"the math, not a vibe\" means — and the one external check that ties it to reality is the zero-false-accusation calibration test.'

8. '73/73 clean wins. No tie.' under a frequency-framed header ('Doesn't this make ties common?') reads as an empirical rate over real use. I reproduced it: it is a per-fallacy separability sweep (73 fallacies with ≥2 tells, deny exactly each one's two own tells and nothing else → 73/73 accuse-self), a best-case existence result, not a rate over real inputs.
   - Tighten doc2 bullet 1 (lines 159–161): 'For the cleanest input — deny exactly one fallacy's own two tells and nothing else — every one of the 73 fallacies separates from its family runner-up by ≥2.5×: 73/73, no built-in tie. This is a per-fallacy separability check (it sweeps the catalog one fallacy at a time), not a rate over real inputs; how often real inputs are this clean is a separate, unmeasured question — and messy inputs route to inconclusive_lean by design (next bullet).' Optionally soften the preface 'Measured against the live catalog' to 'By construction across the live catalog.'

9. 'inconclusive_lean' is glossed as 'an honest non-committal answer' / 'epistemic humility' (doc2 166–167), but it deterministically names one fallacy (leanFallacy: top.fid, scored[0] after sort, engine.js:531–532,546,558). The 'not a coin-flip' contrast is accurate (it IS deterministic) and the docs DO disclose it names the leader, so this is a one-notch wording overclaim, not the 'motivated rebranding/burden-shifting' the objection alleges.
   - Replace doc2 166–167 with: 'A real tie produces a softened, hedged verdict, not a coin-flip: it still names the highest-scoring fallacy as a tentative lean (leanFallacy) but withholds the accusation. The runner-up's score is retained in the beliefs snapshot, so a UI can show the lean is a near-call. That's the goodwill thesis applied to the app itself — humility about confidence (we won't convict on ambiguous evidence), not a claim that we've suspended judgment about which fallacy is most likely.' Do not promise 'we surface the runner-up' as UI behavior — engine.js only returns beliefs:P; display is unverified.

10. doc2's conclusion folds two distinct inconclusive_lean code paths into one reassuring 'genuinely ambiguous argument' with a 'correct' verdict (lines 173–174), while the bullet (163–165) describes only one. Path (a): leader beats innocence but fails the 2.5× runner-up gate (engine.js:555–558). Path (b): NO fallacy beat innocence yet ≥2 distinct fallacies each had a denied tell (engine.js:539,544–546). Case (b) implicated no single fallacy on the app's own gate, so 'correct' papers over a defensible-but-not-forced design choice ('less sound, not sound').
   - Rewrite the bullet and conclusion to name both paths and downgrade 'correct': 'A lean reaches the user in two cases: (1) two fallacies each beat innocence but neither dominates the other by 2.5× (engine.js:555–558) — genuine ambiguity between two live hypotheses; and (2) no single fallacy cleared innocence, but two or more were each independently nicked (engine.js:544–546), which the engine treats as \"less sound, not sound.\" Both yield a hedged lean that names the leader without accusing.' Change 173–174 to: 'So ties aren't a flaw the system stumbles into — every lean that reaches the user is a deliberate hedge, never an accusation. Whether case (2) should lean rather than simply hold up is a defensible design choice, not a logical necessity.'

11. 'it does not pin a label' (doc1 line 148) sits in apposition with 'leans', but a lean does pin a (hedged) label — leanFallacy: top.fid (engine.js:546,558). The rest of the doc is explicit that a lean names X ('might be X', line 124–125; table line 134), so 'label' here loosely means 'confident/accusatory label'.
   - Replace line 148's clause with: 'When in doubt, it holds up or leans — and a lean names the leading fallacy only as a hedged \"might be X, not sure,\" never as a confident accusation.'

12. doc2's NEW-fallacy recipe (lines 139–140) mandates '2–4 questions only it owns … Two so the two-denials-convict target is reachable' without flagging the under-detection consequence: a fallacy genuinely decisive on a single marker (flat self-contradiction, non-sequitur) cannot trip on one tell under this recipe and will read as 'holds up'. The framing half of objection 14 is a misread — the doc already frames the weights as engineering/calibration throughout (lines 6–9, 69, 93–95, 146–148), so do NOT add a 'frame it as convenience' edit.
   - Add after line 140: 'This two-tell floor is a calibration default, not a claim that every fallacy has two independent markers. A fallacy that is genuinely decisive on a single feature (e.g. a flat self-contradiction or non-sequitur) will under-fire under this recipe unless its single decisive feature is split into two ownable tells or given its own stronger treatment — the floor trades some sensitivity on single-marker forms for the never-accuse-a-fallacy-free-argument guarantee.' (The degree-fallacy over-confidence worry needs no fix — the runner-up gate already routes contested cases to inconclusive_lean.)

## Defensible as-is (NOT changed, to avoid over-hedging)

1. The scare-quoted legal idiom 'innocent until proven otherwise' is standard usage (cf. null-hypothesis / presumption-of-validity talk) and, flagged with quotes, misleads no one about engine behavior — the strong 'smuggles a category error' charge is over-reading; only the line-32 literalizing prose needs softening.
2. 'the math, not a vibe' (doc2 line 69) is a defensible contrast between a derivation (target + gate ⇒ weights by arithmetic) and eyeballing; the doc already disclaims per-number pedigree (lines 146–148) and names the external check. Do NOT delete it — deleting would over-hedge a true claim.
3. The doc's overall framing that the weights are role-based calibration artifacts, not per-fallacy fine-tuning (doc2 6–11, 146–148), is sound and already self-aware; objection 14's 'presents uniformity as principled fallacy ontology' is a misread of the text.
4. 'not a coin-flip' (doc2) is literally true — scoreChecklist is deterministic (sorts by score, returns top.fid; no RNG). Only 'non-committal'/'epistemic humility' overstate by one notch; the contrast itself is accurate and should stay.
5. The docs do NOT claim ties are rare in real use, nor that the per-fallacy scores form a single joint distribution, nor that a lean withholds the fallacy name — the preface scopes clean-vs-messy (doc2 154–155), Step 2's 'sums to 100' is scoped to the sequential interview, and lean is explicitly 'might be X'. So the heavier versions of objections 8, 10, 15, 17 (burden-shifting, hidden joint distribution, suspended-judgment) are rejected; only the named wording tightenings apply.
6. Renaming the VALID hypothesis to NO_FALLACY_FOUND / NOT_DEFEATED is the WRONG fix and should not be done — it would erase the real valid_earned (≥2 affirmed virtues, positively justified) vs cynic_valid (merely not-defeated) distinction the engine encodes (engine.js:489–493).
7. doc1 line 124's 'never sound' is the doc correctly declining to claim soundness for a lean — careful, not an equivocation; objection 1's citation of it should be disregarded.
8. The 'beats innocence' half of the court analogy is a genuine standard-of-proof and maps correctly (60% prior, RATIO_VALID/CHECKLIST_RATIO_VALID); only the runner-up-gate and direction-of-doubt extensions break, so the analogy should be qualified, not removed wholesale.

## Affirmed sound (kept)

1. The core mechanism descriptions are accurate to engine.js: per-fallacy isolated {VALID,F} scoring (scoreFallacy 518–529), the C-1 anti-washout rationale, the triple gate, ratio-based (field-size-invariant) thresholds, and the four-outcome table (valid_earned/cynic_valid/accuse/inconclusive_lean) all match the code.
2. The 'two denials convict, one holds up' calibration is real and reproduces: baseline 4.5/0.3 gives two-denied f/VALID ≈ 0.18–0.28 (accuse) vs the 0.12 gate, exactly as doc2's table (lines 84–88) and the CONFIG comment (engine.js:42–51) state.
3. The 73/73 separability result is real (I reproduced 73/73 clean accuse-self); it just needs to be labeled as separability, not frequency.
4. 'The app never reads your argument. It scores your answers' (doc1 line 11) is accurate and is the honest anchor the soundness-disclaimer fixes build on.
5. doc2's distinctiveness section is admirably careful — it explicitly flags that distinctiveness is NOT enforced by validateBank, names the broad routing-question exceptions, and credits the runner-up gate + calibration test as the real enforcers (lines 112–123). Keep as-is.
6. The 'no false accusations, ever' design priority and its primacy ('weights serve it, not the other way around', doc2 125–128) is the right thesis; only the over-broad 'sound' wording and the wrong-engine citation need correcting, not the principle.
7. doc2's honest disclaimers that the numbers are reverse-engineered to the gate and 'correct because they produce correct verdicts, not because of any individual number's pedigree' (lines 71, 146–148) are exactly the intellectual honesty the rest of the review wants propagated into doc1.
